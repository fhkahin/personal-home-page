About Me

I am a biomedical scientist by training, but my current role is that of a refugee case worker for a charity that supports refugees and asylum seekers in rebuilding their lives and integrating into society. On a day-to-day basis, I assist clients in finding suitable colleges and community centres where they can learn English, as well as helping them with their housing needs and providing them with independent information, advice, and guidance.

As a refugee case worker for a charity that supports refugees and asylum seekers, my role is multi-faceted and involves a range of activities that are geared towards helping clients rebuild their lives and integrate into society. One of the key aspects of my role is providing welfare support to clients so that they can access benefits and other financial support. Many of our clients are new to the country and may not be familiar with the different systems and processes that exist. As a result, my job involves providing guidance and support to help them navigate these systems and access the resources that they are entitled to.

In addition to my work as a case worker, I also have a passion for technology and am excited about the potential for coding to transform lives. I discovered Code Your Future while researching services for my clients, and I was thrilled to find an organization that aligns perfectly with my personal values and goals. Helping refugees and asylum seekers to rebuild their lives and integrate into society is something that I have always wanted to do, and coding is my dream.

I am eager to learn programming and use my skills to make a positive impact in the world. I am committed to continuous learning and development and excited to apply my transferable skills and experience as a refugee case worker to excel in the tech sector.
